Hello Debra, Donna and Deidre,

Must say I'm feeling a little out of the "D" loop - perhaps I need a new name to belong to this group... Never the less I will keep going.

I'm a teacher librarian, fairly new to the world of teacher librarianship so I still have much to learn. I teach in an all girls secondary school in Sydney.

Copyright is an area where I feel I need to improve my knowledge as teachers and students constantly ask for advice, particularly with reference to the use of digital media when students are creating their own responses to assignment tasks using Web 2.0 tools. When I came across this course I thought it might help me come to grips with copyright rules, and that by doing this course on line, over an extended time period, the information might actually "sink in"! I've done two copyright PD sessions, but there was so much to cover I left feeling more confused.

I am familiar with Skype and have it set up for use at home. Unfortunately I can't use it at school, so I hope you don't mind collaborating after school hours?

I look forward to hearing from you all over the next week or so.

Warm regards

Jenny (Denny!)

A way of working

Debra Russell
Mon, 2010-03-22 03:37

How about this suggestion for a way of working.

Someone could answer the questions as best they can in Word and email this to the other two. They could add their suggestions and comments to the answers in, say, a different colour and email them back to the first person.

We could then all have a Skype session discussing the answers and formulating the final responses.

The original author would then make the agreed changes and post the assignment.

The following week, another person would do the initial work.

I have some time at the moment and have started to put my answers together so I would be happy to start the process if that sounds like a plan. If you don't like this suggestion, how do you think it might best work?
