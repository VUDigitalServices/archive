1. Pacific Standard Time (GMT -7)
2. I thought of a game the other evening:
Goal: Spread Positivity
The game screen starts in color. As the player walks around, or looks at scenes, certain other 'non-player characters' (NPCs) are emitting greyscale vibrations. The player must cheer these other characters up so that they emit vibrant color (more vibrant than the default scene coloration.)

As the player continues to explore s/he encounters additional objects including TVs and newspaper stands that are draining the surrounding color from people. Certain angry NPCs emit 'redscale' hues while extremely sad NPCs might emit 'bluescale' hues.

The player has several tools by default:
* Smile
* Wink
* Poem
* Joke
* Hug

Throughout the game the player can acquire new tools/techniques such as:
* Universal TV Remote
* Dance
* Flower

I think that this game can help people to be more conscientious of our surroundings and to be more sensitive to emotional vibrations.

Hello everyone, I am Gurmit in Geneva and here is my short essay below, great to meet all of you and read your wonderful creative ideas!

What does the evolution of consciousness entail for me?

While the evolution of consciousness encompasses far too many perspectives for a coherent yet flexible framework, my experiences and insights as a learning technology researcher prompt me to develop a conceptual model to realize this purpose. This should, at the very least, entail and catalyse the ongoing reflection dialogues necessary for positive mindset and behaviour change effects to be sustained as conscious evolutionary action research processes in a global learning community. In addition, we may consider if it should entail a deliberate strategy of raising consciousness using games and apps in distributed networks, so as to improve the impact of educational technology innovations.

At the same time however, we face the equal challenge of shifting attention and resources towards creating fresh curricula and materials to promote literacy in cosmic consciousness. Currently, the absence of ‘consciousness literacy’ precludes the ability of humans to participate in building the Global Brain as a hive of permanent evolution and not merely a static repository of codified knowledge. As the industrial education system collapses, the question then becomes how do we effectively seed and propagate the evolution of consciousness cheaply and pervasively? How do we make the Networked Digital World evolve consciously?

I hope this course will develop a systematic approach to the evolution of consciousness, by mapping targets, stakeholders, themes, and design principles. My added value would be my experience having developed an e-mentoring programme as
as a pedagogic strategy, building on research that informal and social learning is now a recognized way to improve educational outcomes for all. I would like to develop an app called i-Mentor for promoting peer2peer mentoring dialogues by overcoming time and distance barriers to raising the consciousness of our planet.

Thanks!

Hi, all!

Isn't it a busy forum! As I was struggling at work, I kept receiving emails with updates - finally, I can focus on what I really want to discuss about with you guys.

Gurmit, that's almost-Spanish, it's Portuguese. And I'm sorry I haven't come up with an English version yet.

Still, here's the story: Kino is a game where you play as yourself in a world that leaves you room for travelling through different subjects and skills; it is almost a gamelike curriculum. One of the core objectives and studies was towards the 'learning process' in a way that takes mind, body and environment into account. So, you're a student that, to wander about the map (and through the 'master-houses' - teachers, so to speak) you had to *really* wander. Want to go to the Mathematician House? Sure. It's three 'spaces' away and he (the math teach) is also a Kung Fu master, and he'll be sure that your body will learn something worth those three spaces marked on the course map. The masters would always have to have some sort of extra-skill that involves body work: an Arts master that is also good at cooking, a History master that is excellent at planting herbs. Something that would always take the student up and out the chair and classroom to learn with her whole being, instead of (a part of) her head.
All had to take place in a game: a space and a time with its own rules, a voluntary agreement to follow the rules, where the rules are: take your chances, don't be shy to have a body and to learn with it, etc, etc.

Uff. Sorry if it is too tiring; it needs to be refined and put to work in small bits. I'm posting some of the references in the Course Material; would any of you have thoughts on this?
