Hello Good People,

We entered the fourth week of the course. The plan is to build on the worfk started in week 3 and widen the scope to ask: How does open governance (or related systems of horizontal governance, self-governance, or commons management) work in the various places in which it is currently found?

The idea was to start a discussion based on the work done the week before.
Now, some people have started a "project", others have not yet.

Personally I suggest that you continue to work on the task started last week, or that you start a project in case you have not done it yet, according to the indications here: http://p2pu.org/general/node/15762/forums/26115.

Please post your comments in the blog, as usual.

Marisa

updating progress on my project: (thanks to Dan for his help with the 909 forms)
* After a quick skim of the Free Software Foundation's 2009 "909" tax form, I found an intriguing aspect of their governance model, that of the 7 highest or "key" employees, only Executive Director Peter Brown received a salary, of $79,602. Nobody else got paid (from page 9).
** Thus the majority of the work done by the board is free. This implies a very high motivaiton for their board, something I would *assume* is not true for most non-profits and/or organizations in general. I'd tend to think this would make internal governance far more efficient, 'cause if you're not getting paid and still putting in the time you'd be highly unlikely to waste your time.
